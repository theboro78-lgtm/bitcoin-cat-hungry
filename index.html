Here Comes Bitcoin Cat (HUNGRY)

Bitcoin was not created to be owned.
Bitcoin was created as an exit from the old system.

Yet today, Bitcoin sits neatly on balance sheets.
Owned by large corporations.
Accumulated by major institutions.
Managed by entities that already have more than enough.

They are full.
Yet still hungry.

—

Bitcoin Cat exists as a marker of this shift.

A cat that looks well-fed.
Heavy.
Already satisfied by any reasonable measure.
Yet still searching.

This is not a visual accident.
This is not a design mistake.

—

There is no error on the part of the developer who embedded this digital artifact.
On the contrary, it is an intentional and honest representation of the current condition.

The evidence lives directly in the code itself:

hungry.json
animation-hungry
herecomesbitcoin-hungry

The word “hungry” was chosen, repeated, and preserved—
not as a playful name,
but as a state.

A state of hunger that does not come from scarcity,
but from persistent dissatisfaction,
even in excess.

—

Bitcoin was born to avoid control.
Today, Bitcoin’s price moves at the hands of players large enough
to dictate market rhythm.

Volatility is no longer chaos.
It is strategy.

Scarcity is no longer liberation.
It has become inventory.

Bitcoin did not fail.
Its direction drifted.

—

Bitcoin Cat (HUNGRY) is not a tribute.
It is not a solution.
It does not offer repair.

It is a mirror.

A mirror of what happens
when a system designed to starve intermediaries
ends up feeding them instead.

The cat is not thin.
It is fat.
And still hungry.

—

Bitcoin Cat does not exist to fix Bitcoin.
It cannot.

It exists to mark a moment—
when original intent and present reality
no longer move in parallel.

A Hidden Digital Artifact,
embedded in code,
discovered through curiosity,
and left to speak for itself.

—

Bitcoin still has its protocol.
Markets still have their cycles.

But hunger has changed hands.

And Bitcoin Cat (HUNGRY)
is what it looks like.
